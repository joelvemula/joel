Supplier Profile


The View Supplier page displays all of the Supplier provided data and also allows the Buyer to perform actions and view 3rd party information related to this Supplier. The screen has three main tabs at the top of the page for navigation.

  • Profile - View all of the data published by the Supplier including Profile Information, Questionnaire responses and Banking information
  • Installed Apps - View 3rd party information related to this Supplier
  • Action Center - View relationship history, manage approvals and checklists, view or update Custom Field values, add ERP system ID's, etc.

    The Supplier Status and the Last Updated date are shown in the Top Right banner of the page.

    Profile
    The Supplier Profile tab is organized into the following panels. Each panel can be collapsed or expanded with the icon in the top right corner.
    Expand each topic below for more information.

    Overview Buyers can view a Supplier's basic profile information in the Overview section, including:
    • Company description
    • Legal business name
    • Date established
    • Tax Country for this entity
    • Website (optional; field does not display if supplier does not enter website address)
    • Email address and Corporate Phone Number
    • Industries (NAICS)
    • Stock Exchange and Symbol
    • Number of full-time and part-time employees
    • Stock exchange and stock exchange symbol (if applicable)
    • VAT or other registration number and document (if applicable)
    • UNSPSC product codes
    • Business type
    • Tax and Corporate identifiers and Documents


    Liaisons
    Buyers can monitor the status of the Email invitation in the Liaisons panel of the Supplier’s Profile. Until an invitation is Accepted, this is the only panel available on the Profile tab. The Liaison section displays the Name and email address to which the invitation was sent along with the date time stamp. If the invitation was Accepted, the date and time will be shown. If an email was rejected, opened, or delivered, it is noted here. Email statuses you may see on the page are:
    • ProcessedMessage has been received by Send grid and is ready to be delivered
    • Dropped You may see the following drop reasons:
      • Invalid SMTP API header
      • Spam Content (if Spam Checker app is enabled)
      • Unsubscribed Address
      • Bounced Address
      • Spam Reporting Address
      • Invalid
      • Recipient List over Package Quota
    • Delivered Message has been successfully delivered to the receiving server.
    • Deferred Receiving server temporarily rejected the message.
    • Bounce Receiving server could not or would not accept the message. If a recipient has previously unsubscribed from your emails, the message is bounced.
    • Open Recipient has opened the HTML message. Open Tracking needs to be enabled for this type of event.
    • Click Recipient clicked on a link within the message. Click Tracking needs to be enabled for this type of event.
    • Spam Report Recipient marked message as spam.
    Some email statuses are blocked by the recipient’s email server so you may not see an "Open" or "Click" status for every email address. If statuses are blocked, the status remains at "Delivered"

    Send an invite to a new Liaison
    If your Supplier is still in Pending status and the invited contact does not respond or the email is undeliverable, you may send an invitation to a new Liaison. Click the Invite New Liaison button and fill in the required information to send the invite. Click Next. Review the summary page and click Send Invite on the next screen or the Back button to make any modifications to the information.

    Resend an invitation
    You can resend an invitation by clicking on the blue Resend Email Invite button in the Liaisons panel. This should be used if a supplier has not responded to the invite by creating a user ID. This action sends a reminder email to the invitee and encourages them to join TYS per your request. You can resend an invite up to nine times.
    A confirmation message states when the email notification has been sent successfully.
    Note: Once an invite has been accepted, the Supplier must add any new users themselves. Please refer these requests to the Liaison you see in Accepted status in this pane.
    Contacts
    View Supplier executive contacts such as the CEO, CFO, and Director of Sales on the Contacts panel. Any additional contacts that the Supplier has entered will also be shown here. Each contact is listed on a separate "page" with the ability to sort the names by importance of role or alphabetically.
    Year of Birth is an optional field that will not display if the supplier did not enter it.

    Financials
    View the Supplier’s basic financial information in the Financials panel. A graph and table depicting the last three years of revenue, liability, profit and assets are displayed.

    Ownership Information
    View Ownership information of the supplier company in the Ownership panel. It displays pie graph based on ownership type (individual, parent company, ultimate parent company or entity/organization).

    Locations
    View the supplier's locations including the Domestic HQ on a map by scrolling to the Locations panel. Use the navigation buttons to view additional addresses.

    Corporate Relationships
    The Corporate Relationships panel has two tabs:
    • TYS - displays companies that are a part of the supplier's corporate group, along with the status of the invitation of each entity invited to join that group (optional).
    • GLEIF - provides the parent and child organizations of a supplier if the supplier has a GLEIF ID updated in TYS. The GLEIF ID is fetched from GLEIF using the supplier's Business Name, City, and Country that they entered for their headquarters location. More information about GLEIF.org can be found here: https://www.gleif.org/en/about/this-is-gleif.


    Questionnaires
    Review the status of all questionnaires you have assigned to a supplier on the Questionnaires panel.

    Incomplete questionnaires have a "Pending" tag. If a supplier has completed and published a questionnaire, you may click on View Answers next to the questionnaire title to expand it and see the responses.

    The questions and answers display below the questionnaire name. Note that any automatically flagged questionnaire responses generate a buyer Approval Request (viewable in the supplier profile Action Center tab).

    To hide the questions and answers again, click on the Hide Answers action button. To download them in an Excel sheet, click on Download. Clicking on Download All gives two options to download all questionnaires to Excel, with or without their answers.
    If the question requested a contact, that person's information displays under the question (and also in the supplier profile Contacts panel).

    Use the Add Note button to add a note or attachment to a response. The note is only visible to the buyer.

    Clicking on Manage/Assign at the top right corner of the panel brings up the Assign Questionnaires screen, where you can add or remove questionnaires for the Supplier.You can assign additional questionnaires at any point in the process, even after a supplier has been onboarded. A notification will be sent to the supplier informing them a new questionnaire has been assigned to them.

    Documents
    The Documents section lists all documents uploaded by the supplier and published to the TYS network as a part of their questionnaire responses or profile publish. This is a one-stop place to search, view, and download any of the supplier’s documents. It also indicates any expiry dates associated with the document, if applicable.

    Filters are available above the listing to filter the documents by status (expired) or by source (section or questionnaire where document was originally uploaded).

    Download any supplier document by clicking the 3 vertical dots at the end of the row.

    Documents with a red Expiration Date have expired, and documents in Amber are close to expiration (within 30-60 days).

    Supplier Questionnaire Group The Supplier Questionnaire Group panel allows you to view any previously assign Questionnaire group(s) or Add/Remove them.

    The assigned group(s) display and can be removed by clicking on the appropriate 'x' This removes all questionnaires associated with that questionnaire group from that supplier.

    To add a group, click on the Select Questionnaire Group drop-down. This displays the list of available questionnaire groups. Choose the group you wish to add by clicking on it.

    Remember to click on the blue Save Changes button to save any deleted or added groups.

    Bank Accounts
    The Bank Accounts section panel displays any Supplier or Factoring Company bank accounts that the Supplier has shared with your buyer company. Users with permission to view the Bank Information will be able to click the card and view the complete information. Trust Your Supplier is not a transactional system, however, it provides this secure location for Suppliers to safely share the account information. The banking information supplied here can be used to set up this Supplier's ACH/EFT information in your Accounts Payable system.

Some Apps have pay-on-demand licenses which will prompt for payment when used. Most Apps have a free offering to trial or view limited information.

    Action Center
    Once your supplier has completed their profile and any assigned questionnaires, it will be published for review and your organization's internal due diligence including any Checklists you have on TYS and Approval Requests that may have been generated. These actions are managed on the Action Center tab. Once those activities are complete, the system will let you Onboard the Supplier.
    You can perform the following actions from the supplier's profile:

    Approval Requests You must address any pending Approval Requests before a Supplier can be onboarded. Approval requests are automatically generated based on responses given to questionnaires. Rules for what responses should generate Approvals are configured by your administrator.

    Approval requests are segregated in a tabular format that includes:
    1. My Team (only requests relevant to your role)
    2. All (all requests for the buyer organization)
    3. Closed (requests will move to this status after onboarding)

    The Approval Request table displays one AR per row, with the key information shown such as where the Approval Request generated from, what role is assigned, status and key dates. While users can view all approval requests in the All tab, they can only act upon those that are assigned to their role. If your role is not responsible for this approval, your actions are limited to view-only. Click on the row to open the AR and view the details and take actions. The screen will display the reason the Approval was generated and also show the question that was posed and how the supplier answered. Any prior history with this Approval is shown in the table at the bottom of the screen.
    Several actions can be taken on an Approval Requests.
    • Approve
    • Reject
    • Re-Assign - transfer request and approval permission to another role
    • Info Request - reassigns without approval permission, and returns to approver
    • Add Note - add internal notes to approval requests
      Approve and Reject buttons are shown on the top right. The additional actions are in the vertical ellipsis. Click the ellipsis to open the menu for additional actions.
      Clicking on the Approve or Reject option opens a right panel. You have the option to add an internal note and attach a file before you reject or approve the request.

      To reassign a request to a different role, select the Re-assign option.

      Once you click the Re-assign button, a pop-up window appears. A note or document can be attached if desired. A drop-down selector is shown to choose the role to which the approval request should be assigned. Reassigning the approval request will transfer the approval authority to that role. Click Re-Assign to complete the action.

      Users can also create a manual approval request by clicking the New Approval Request button. This opens the Create Approval Request panel where the user can enter a description of the requested action, attach a file, and select the role to be assigned the action. Click Create to save the Approval.



    View Relationship History
    You can view the entire history on TYS with this Supplier in the Relationship History panel. The timeline events include:

    1. Invitation Sent
    2. Questionnaires Assigned
    3. Invitation Accepted
    4. Profile Complete
    5. Questionnaire Published
    6. (Profile) Ready for Review
    7. Returned to Supplier
    8. Onboarded

    The default view of the history is a Timeline view. Use the left arrow (<) and the right arrow (>) to navigate the timeline. You can also click the Table icon to switch your view to a table listing of the events.
    Events include any buyer and supplier actions but there are also system or platform related events. Click the Eyeball next to Include System events to include those events in your timeline or table.

    Checklist Questionnaires (Internal Due Diligence)
    Formerly called Internal Due Diligence Questionnaires (IDD), Checklist Questionnaires are used by buyers internally, to ensure their internal checks and diligence actions have been completed before onboarding. As with any other questionnaire, approval requests may be generated based on responses to these questions.

    The Checklist Questionnaire panel appears when the supplier publishes their profile Click "Begin" besides each Checklist Questionnaire to begin answering the questions. Checklist Questionnaires must be completed along with all Approvals before a supplier can be onboarded

    Answer all the questions and click the Submit button. Notes and attachments can be added and are only visible internally. Checklist Questionnaires can be saved while in progress. If a Questionnaire is in progress, you will click Continue to resume it. Click Submit after completing the questionnaire.

    Upon successful submission, the questionnaire is updated with the date-time stamp. You can click the Update button to edit or update the answers after submitting.

    The questions in the checklists are established by the Administrator and Process Owner and are the same for every supplier.

    To export completed Questionnaires, click the Download All button at the top of the panel. An excel workbook will be downloaded with each Questionnaire on a separate sheet. The filename will be the Supplier name-Internal Due Diligence Questionnaires.

    Buyer System Details The Buyer System Details panel allows the buyer to reference internal system IDs associated with this supplier. These are only viewable by the buyer.

    Click on the Add Buyer System Details button to bring up a new row. Choose your System Name from the drop-down list (determined during buyer set up) and enter a System ID.

    If System IDs from different systems need to be added, click on the Add Buyer System Details button again to create a second row.

    For some buyers, there is no requirement to enter an internal system ID, so the Add Buyer System Details button and System Name and ID fields do not display.

    Notes
    Adding notes is a tool for you to use as a buyer to record internal notes and comments for this supplier. Notes are not visible to the supplier. A date-time stamp is added to each note. To add a note and/or attachment, use the Add Note button.

    Click the Save button to save the notes and attachments.

    If you need to edit your note, click on the Edit button. A pop-up appears for you to make changes.

    After clicking Save from this screen you get a confirmation message stating that the note has been saved.

    Custom Fields
    You have the option to enter custom fields to any supplier record. These fields are customized and specific to each buyer and are only visible to your company and its users.

    Click on Save Changes at the bottom of the panel to save any additions or changes made.



    Notifications

    The Notifications tab displays all Notifications tied to the Supplier. Each notification has a Description, date/time, and a category. Clicking on a row will navigate you to the area of the system associated with that notification. From there you can review information or take action as needed.
    Once clicked, a Notification will be marked as Read automatically. To manually mark a Notification as Read or Unread, use the checkboxes on each row, or the Select All checkbox and then click the Mark as Read or Mark as Unread buttons located above the table. The Select All checkbox will check all Notifications on the page. by clicking on the check box in the upper left.

    The notifications page is enabled with pagination feature for easy retrieval of records. You can change the number of records displayed per page by selecting from the Rows per page drop-down and also navigate to a page using the Page Number drop-down selector at the bottom of the page. Use the Arrows to navigate to the Next Page or Previous Page to navigate between adjacent pages.

    To filter the Notification listing choose the Filter by button to open the filter selection pane. Many filter options are available including:
    • Date
    • Category
    • Read/Unread Status

    To execute the query with your selections, click Search and see the filtered results. To clear your filters while in the panel, click Reset Filter. To see any applied filters while on the main Notification page, you will see what filter categories are applied as buttons left of the Filter By box. To remove one, click the X next to it or use the Clear All link and your results will be adjusted.

    Screen Actions
    Return Profile To The Supplier For Corrections (Optional)
    If there are issues or questions and the supplier cannot be onboarded, you can return the profile to the supplier for more information or corrections. Click the Return to Supplier button in the top right corner of the screen. . A window will appear that contains two free form text boxes. One is visible only internally to you as a buyer, and the Reason for Return box should be used to communicate the issue to the supplier.

    Once the supplier has re-published their profile and marked the action complete, you can continue the onboarding process.

    Status Changes during Return to Supplier feature If the buyer performs a Return to Supplier on a Supplier who is currently under "Ready for Review" status, the supplier profile is moved to an "Action Required" status.

    The buyer can undo this action by clicking on the Undo Return to Supplier button that appears. The supplier moves back to a "Ready for Review" status and the buyer can use the Onboard Supplier button to onboard the supplier.

    Buyers can return a supplier's profile to them when a supplier is already in an "Onboarded" status by clicking on the Return to Supplier button.

    A confirmation pop-up displays:

    This moves the supplier back to an "Action Required" Status. From here, you can use Undo Return to Supplier.

    The supplier now goes back to a "Ready for Review" Status. From here the buyer can onboard the supplier again by clicking the Onboard Supplier button.



    Onboard Supplier Once all approval requests and Checklist Questionnaires are complete, you can onboard the supplier. If you attempt to Onboard a supplier before checklist or approvals are completed, an error message will inform you.
    Click on the blue Onboard Supplier button from the top right corner of the page.

    An Onboard Supplier pop-up window appears.

    You can attach relevant documents or write notes before clicking the Onboard button. Note are for internal use only and are not visible to the supplier.
    Once the onboarding action is successfully completed, the supplier receives a notification and moves to an "Onboarded" status in their profile. A new event is noted on the Relationship History panel.

    Scroll To Top Button
    Each tab of the Supplier has a Scroll to Top button as you scroll towards the bottom of a tab. It moves with the page, staying in the bottom right corner. Click on it to jump back to the top of the tab.

    Use the back facing blue arrow beside the Supplier name in the header areas to return to Connected Suppliers page.
